Better Branding or Art You Truly Love

William-Adolphe Bouguereau stated: "For me a work of art must be an elevated interpretation of nature" and "One shouldn't believe in all those so-called innovations. There is only one nature and only one way to see it. Nowadays, they want to succeed too fast, this is how they go about inventing new aesthetics, pointillism, pipisme! All this just to make noise." Charles Augustus (Shorty) Lasar wrote: "It is the intense liking for a scene that makes it art" and "A fad is a section, while a work of art is complete."

Unfortunately, too many people in the art market have been influenced to purchase art, that deep down, they may truly not love. Whether it is an abstract expressionist movement piece by Jackson Pollock (using his "drip technique" of splashing liquid household paint onto a horizontal surface) selling for $61,161,000 or an Andy Warhol (silkscreen ink and silver spray paint) selling for $105,445,000, we strongly believe you should purchase fine art for one reason – you love it!

"Art is our memory of love. The most an artist can do through their work is say, let me show you what I have seen, what I have loved, and perhaps
you will see it and love it too." - Annie Bevan
